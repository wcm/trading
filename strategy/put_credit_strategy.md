# Put Credit Strategy, Explained Simply

Last updated: 2026-06-10

This explains the current `put_credit_strategy` in plain language. It is one
strategy that runs on the shared bot infrastructure. This is an engineering note
for the paper-trading bot, not financial advice.

Shared infrastructure details live in
[../automatic_trading_bot_infra.md](../automatic_trading_bot_infra.md).

## Tiny Glossary

A **stock** is a tiny piece of a company.

An **option** is like a promise or coupon about a stock price.

A **put option** is protection against a stock going down.

A **put credit spread** is our current strategy:

- We **sell one put**: someone pays us money now.
- We **buy another lower put**: this protects us if things go badly.
- The money we receive is called **credit**.
- Because we buy protection, the worst possible loss is known upfront. This is
  called **defined risk**.

Example:

```text
Sell AAPL 190 put
Buy AAPL 185 put
Receive $1.00 credit
```

Options are for 100 shares, so `$1.00 credit` means about `$100`.

If the gap between strikes is `$5`, worst case is:

```text
($5 width - $1 credit) * 100 = $400 max loss
```

## 1. When Do We Open A Trade?

The cloud bot wakes during US market hours.

The scheduler has two separate jobs:

- **Monitor:** only checks whether existing positions should be closed. This
  runs every minute only when there are open positions.
- **Look for new opportunities:** runs every 5 minutes as long as the account
  risk gates allow new trades.

Before opening anything, it asks:

```text
Are we allowed to open new trades at all?
```

It skips opening if:

- daily loss is too high: `max_daily_loss: 500`
- weekly loss is too high: `max_weekly_loss: 1000`
- too many new trades today: `max_new_trades_per_day: 1`
- total possible open loss would exceed `max_open_risk: 5000`
- account equity falls too low
- kill switch is active
- market data is stale
- Discord is not configured

It also checks the broad market first:

```text
For this strategy, QQQ is the market weather check.
If QQQ is weak, stale, falling too much today, or below its trend line,
the bot should not open new put credit spreads.
```

Then for each stock in the watchlist:

```text
QQQ, AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, AMZN, META, GOOGL, TSLA
```

the bot builds possible put credit spreads.

It only considers spreads with:

- **14-30 DTE**: expiration is 14 to 30 days away
- **Delta -0.10 to -0.18**: farther away from the current stock price
- **Short put distance at least 3%**: the danger line must be at least 3%
  below the current stock price
- **Spread width 5 or 10**: the strike gap is $5 or $10
- enough **liquidity**: enough people trading it
- enough **credit**: at least 10% of the spread width
- fresh option quotes
- market trend passing
- earnings not too close, if known

Then the bot runs a hard pre-LLM filter.

This happens before fetching news and before calling the LLM.

The bot skips the LLM if:

- no option candidates passed the scanner
- the latest stock bar is stale
- the stock trend filter is failing
- QQQ broad-market filter is failing
- earnings/event filter is failing
- candidate max loss is above the per-trade limit
- option quotes are stale
- the short put is too close to the current stock price

Plain English:

```text
If the numbers are bad, do not ask the AI to talk us into a trade.
```

Only after the hard filters pass does the bot fetch news and call the LLM.

Then the LLM receives:

- stock price data
- option candidates
- Greeks like Delta, Gamma, Theta, and Vega
- market trend
- account state
- current open positions
- recent news

The LLM can say:

```text
open / skip / hold / close / disable_trading
```

But it cannot invent trades. It must choose from trades generated by code.

The LLM must also be confident enough:

```text
If confidence is below 0.80, the bot rejects the open decision.
```

Plain English:

```text
When the setup is not obviously good, skip.
```

## 2. How Much And What Do We Open?

Currently, the bot only opens:

```text
put credit spreads
```

No naked options. No calls. No market orders.

For each trade, current v1 quantity is:

```text
1 spread
```

So even if the LLM is very confident, it cannot say "buy 10 spreads" right now.

But the bot can open multiple separate trades over time if:

- each one passes validation
- no new trade has already been opened today
- total open max loss stays under `$5,000`
- no other risk gate blocks it

After all LLM decisions come back, the allocator ranks accepted trades by:

1. LLM confidence, as long as it is at least 0.80
2. reward/risk ratio
3. max profit
4. lower max loss
5. symbol tie-breaker

Then it picks the best eligible one.

Before submitting, the bot refreshes the option quotes again. If the price got
worse or stale, it refuses.

Orders are submitted as **limit orders**, meaning:

```text
Only trade at my chosen price or better.
```

No **market orders**, meaning the bot will not say:

```text
Just fill me at whatever price.
```

That is important because options can have bad prices.

## 3. When Do We Close?

Closing is currently mostly rule-based, not fully LLM-based yet.

When there are open option positions, the bot monitors them every minute.

It reconstructs the spread from the option legs and checks:

1. **Profit target hit**

   If we already made about 50% of the original credit, close.

   Example: we received `$100`. If we can buy it back for `$50`, we keep about
   `$50` profit.

2. **Loss trigger hit**

   If closing cost becomes about 2x the original credit, close/recommend close.

   Example: we received `$100`. If it now costs `$200` to close, that is a
   danger sign.

3. **Short strike threatened**

   If the stock falls to or below the short put strike, close/recommend close.

   Example: we sold the `190P`. If stock falls to `$190` or lower, danger.

4. **Expiration too close**

   If only 3 days or fewer remain, close/recommend close.

The bot should not intentionally hold spreads through expiration.

Right now, close monitoring does **not yet deeply use news/LLM judgment** the
same way opening does. That is a known next improvement.

## Simple Summary

Opening:

```text
Every 5 minutes during market hours:
  account risk gates pass?
  QQQ broad-market weather check passes?
  scan watchlist
  generate put credit spreads
  remove candidates too close to danger
  hard numeric filters pass?
  if no, skip without news or LLM
  fetch news
  give candidates + news to LLM
  validate hard rules
  pick best trade
  recheck fresh quotes
  submit 1-spread limit order
```

Sizing:

```text
1 spread per trade
max 1 new trade/day
max total open risk $5,000
```

Closing:

```text
Every minute if positions exist:
  close if profit target hit
  close if loss trigger hit
  close if stock touches short strike
  close if near expiration
```
